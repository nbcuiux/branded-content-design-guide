Introduction

NBCUXLab is a group of user-centric problem solvers who architect culturally relevant products with innovation at the core of our strategy. We saw the need for a new avenue that allows brands to reach their audiences better. Our solution, V.ai, aims to combine branded content with product integration in the video space in order to shape the future of how brands influence consumers while delivering an immersive video-viewing experience.

V.ai Origins

H ave you ever watched a show online and gotten annoyed by the seemingly endless commercials? Did you download an ad blocker plugin to your browser? More likely than not, you did.

It’s been estimated that if ad-blocking rates maintain at their current levels, then advertisers will lose $35 billion by 2020. According to a new report from PageFair, a company that helps advertisers regain revenue lost to ad blocking software, ad blocker usage surged 30% in 2016. By the end of 2016, there were 615 million devices blocking ads worldwide.

After conducting a survey, PageFair found that the main reason U.S. consumers downloaded an ad blocker was due to virus and malware concerns (30%). The second top motivation was to prevent interruptions (29%).

We’re really focused on keeping up with the current digital landscape; upon review of Amazon Prime Video, we noticed how they used their X-Ray feature as a vehicle to advertise music. This novel approach piqued our curiosity.

In the current digital space, one stymied by ad blockers and full of displeased consumers, the question of how to move forward in advertising remains unanswered. The traditional way of advertising can no longer withstand the test of time. Users are changing.

Streaming services with minimal ad content are being chosen over conventional cable providers. We began researching how different leading brands are dealing with the emerging advertising problem. More importantly, how are they working to sponsor brands without reverting to the traditional advertising protocol?

Upon diving deeper into Amazon Prime Video, we thought that they took a unique approach in answering this question. By incorporating music identification into X-Ray, Amazon led users to the purchasing space without infringing on the videoviewing experience. Good advertising is advertising you don’t notice at all. Inspired by this approach, we came up with our solution to the problem: V.ai.

Shopping & Products

V.ai uses artificial intelligence to identify specific products in a video.

After identifying a product in the video (like that cute scarf the lead actress is wearing), V.ai allows users to easily access the purchasing space without encroaching on the viewing experience.

This feature offers a new edge in the realm of advertising.

Brands can now sponsor T.V. shows and movies to promote their products in a new and innovative way without using traditional commercials that interrupt and upset consumers.

T.V. show fans can now shop for the same deerstalker hat made famous by Benedict Cumberbatch in Sherlock—all without even needing to tab away from the video.

Actors & Characters

Besides addressing the goal of advertising, V.ai aims to elevate the video viewing experience. Let’s be honest, most of us have pulled out our phones to google the charismatic protagonist in the middle of a movie before.

Now, by merging character and actor information into the video player, we’ve eliminated the need to open a new tab or whip out another device to answer that irking question that will keep us up at night: What else have I seen that actress in?

Music

We’ve all had that moment where we’ve struggled to turn on Shazam or Siri before the music ends to ask: What song is this?

Music is a part of the video watching experience that people are interested in and, to be quite frank, we’re living during a time where opening and waiting for an app to listen to a song is simply too much work.

Consumers want their information and they want it fast. By automatically identifying music for users, we’re making their lives easier and we’re eliminating any unnecessary obstacles with a seamless experience.

Scenes

Scene selection is another important feature we integrated into our player. Not sure when Leslie and Ben get married in that episode of Parks and Recreation?

Rather than manually scanning the timeline, scene selection allows you to quickly jump to that specific scene. You don’t need to know when; you just need to scan the scene titles.

Trivia

We also decided to incorporate pieces of trivia into V.ai. People love to collect snippets of information about the shows they watch.

Their friends will also be wildly amazed by how much arcane knowledge they have about cult favorites during trivia night. We think it will provide a unique, quirky angle that will encourage consumers to open the overlay.

Wishlists & Starring

Once the overlay is open, our array of advertising features is no more than a glance away. Advertising and e-commerce go hand in hand. We wanted to know what creates an enjoyable online shopping experience.

Our hefty research revealed that wishlists are one of the most important e-commerce features nowadays. Not only are wishlists vital for increasing sales by driving traffic, but they also dramatically improve the consumer experience.

Users want an opportunity to save items and easily return to them later. This is especially important when they’re watching their favorite show and don’t want any interruptions (yet they still want to buy the lead actress’s sunglasses).

Wishlists save precious browsing time and act as a handy, personalized checklist. We were inspired by the functionality of wishlists and aspired to go beyond.

We wanted to allow users to save not only products and songs, but we wanted users to get the chance to save characters and actors they want to read more about later.

Too caught up in the scandals to look up what else you’ve seen that actress in? Just star the actress to go back after the episode. The "starring" feature we’ve built into the player is a perfect example of how V.ai marries the e-commerce and video viewing experience.
